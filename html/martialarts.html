Second Dan Black Belt

January 15, 2022
Sarah Heinen, Grandmaster Perez, Peter Heinen
Board members and promoted students

After two years as a first degree black belt, I tested for and earned a second degree on my black belt. It is the next step in the journey of ten degrees. I completed this achievement under Grandmaster Sam Perez, at Denison Family Karate Center.

This test was similar to my first degree black belt test. We were tested both physically and on our knowledge. We completed a set number of exercises before the test began. We demonstrated every form we’d learned up through first degree black belt, as well as recited the definitions and meanings of the Korean names of these forms. We showed the kicks, blocks, and self-defense we’d learned up through first degree black belt. Lastly, we sparred four opponents, taking each down while defending against the others.

On the board were Grandmaster Keith Yates, Grandmaster Larry Wheeler, Grandmaster Sam Perez, Grandmaster Caroline Goodspeed, and six other black belts in A-KaTo (American Karate and Taekwondo Organization).

First Dan Black Belt

September 21, 2019
Peter Heinen, Grandmaster Perez, Sarah Heinen

I earned my first dan black belt in Taekwondo at Denison Family Karate Center. This is the first of ten degrees of black belt. The Japanese and Korean names for first degree black belt mean "first step". It is a goal many people seek, but it is only the beginning of a lifelong journey. After six years of training, I earned the "first step" in my black belt journey.

The belt test consists of both physical challenges and tests of knowledge. Before the belt test began, were required to complete a set number of exercises. During the test, we demonstrated what we learned in the years leading up to our first degree black belt. This included forms, kicks, and self-defense, as well as definitions of Korean terms and history of Taekwondo. We also demonstrated our sparring and takedown abilities against four opponents.
